The Prism

One conversation. Several tuned lenses. A map of what you couldn't see.

The Prism is LUMA's first analytical instrument. It takes a single recorded conversation with the person who runs your business and processes it through several calibrated lenses — each examining a different dimension of how your organization actually operates. What comes back is not a summary. It's a map of territory you've been walking through blind.

How it unfolds

The engagement has four steps. The first is context. The second is yours. The third is the instrument's. The fourth is ours together.

15 min

The Baseline

Before we talk, we ask for whatever you already have — an org chart, a brand guide, role descriptions, process documents. Whatever exists. Many businesses don't have much, and that's fine. The absence is as informative as the presence.

If you have high-level financials or year-over-year numbers you're willing to share, they sharpen the analysis considerably. But nothing is required. This isn't homework. It's context.

A short form and a place to upload documents. Takes about 15 minutes if you have things ready. Less if you don't.

60 min

The Overview

A single conversation with a LUMA practitioner. Sixty minutes. No preparation required beyond showing up ready to talk honestly about how things actually work — not the polished version, the real one.

This is not an interview. There is no questionnaire. You talk about your business the way you'd talk to someone you trust who asked the right questions. Vision, frustrations, history, operations, the hard parts, the parts that work, the parts you've stopped thinking about.

The conversation is recorded with your permission. The recording is the raw material.

1–3 days

The Analysis

Your conversation is processed through the Prism — several analytical lenses, each calibrated to a different dimension of your operations. Each lens examines the same conversation from a different angle, surfaces different patterns, and produces findings grounded in your own words.

The lenses don't summarize what you said. They surface what you revealed without realizing it — the decision logic you've never articulated, the friction you've stopped noticing, the historical lessons still shaping your present, the gap between where you're headed and where you said you were going.

30 min

The Readout

A brief walkthrough of what the analysis surfaced. Not a presentation — a focused look at the most important findings and what they suggest about your business. You'll see your operations mapped in a way you've never seen them before.

The deliverable is yours to keep regardless of what you decide next. A multi-resolution document where you see the strategic picture and your team sees operational detail — the same analysis, self-selecting depth. Every finding grounded in direct quotes from your conversation.

What the lenses examine

Each lens is calibrated to a dimension of your operations that is typically invisible — not because it's hidden, but because no one has had a reason to look. The same conversation yields different findings through each lens.

Decision Logic

How you actually make decisions — the hierarchy of criteria you use instinctively but have never articulated.

Signal Clarity

Where communication degrades between layers. Mixed signals, workarounds, and friction nobody notices anymore.

Institutional Memory

The historical lessons still shaping how you operate — and which rules may have outlived their original context.

Role Distribution

Who actually does what. Where one person carries six roles. Where critical work has no owner.

Direction of Travel

Where you said you're going versus where your decisions, time, and investment suggest you're actually headed.

Market Perception

How you see your business versus how your own stories suggest it's experienced by clients and the market.

Financial Shape

The relative shape of how your business earns and spends — not a financial audit, but the picture that emerges from how you talk about money.

What you receive

A single document built for two audiences at once. You read it at strategic altitude — where the business is headed, what's working, what's quietly breaking. Your team reads it at operational depth — who does what, where things get stuck, which processes need attention. Same analysis. Self-selecting resolution.

Inside the deliverable

Findings across every analytical dimension, each grounded in direct quotes from your conversation. Not generalized advice — specific observations about your business, in your language, based on what you said.

Where stated priorities diverge from revealed behavior — framed as a mirror, not a verdict. Where friction exists that nobody's measuring. Where historical lessons are still serving you and where they're quietly costing you. Where the vision and the direction of travel have drifted apart.

Every finding includes a confidence rating. Where we're reading between the lines, we say so. Where we'd want to go deeper, we tell you what we'd ask next.
