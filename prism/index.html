The Prism

One conversation. Seven perspectives. A map of territory you’ve been walking through blind.

The conversation

It starts with sixty minutes. One recorded conversation between you and a trained facilitator. No prep required. No documents to send in advance. Just talk about your work the way you actually think about it.

The conversation moves through three phases. The first fifteen minutes establish context — what your business does, who’s involved, how things are structured. The next thirty-five minutes go deeper — how decisions actually get made, where things break down, what keeps you up. The last ten minutes are a reflection — you hear back what the conversation revealed, in real time.

That’s the only thing asked of you. One hour. Honest answers.

What happens next

The recording is processed through multiple calibrated perspectives. Each one listens to the same conversation, but each one is tuned to a different dimension of how your business actually operates — decision logic, communication patterns, role distribution, historical context, trajectory, self-perception, financial shape.

They don’t summarize. They surface what’s there but invisible — the patterns embedded in how you describe things, the gaps between what you say matters and what your decisions suggest matters, the work being done that no one has counted.

What you receive

A written document. Not a slide deck. Not a dashboard. A structured, readable analysis of your business from multiple distinct angles, grounded in your own words.

Every finding is supported by direct quotes from the conversation. Nothing is fabricated. Nothing is generic. The document describes your business, not a category of businesses.

You also receive a synthesis — where the perspectives converge, where they contradict each other, and what the full picture suggests that no single angle could see alone.

What it costs

One hour of your time. The analysis is delivered within one week.

If what you read doesn’t describe your business more clearly than anything you’ve seen before, it didn’t work. That’s the standard.
